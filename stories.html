Zerodha, founded by Nikhil Kamath in 2010, started as a discount brokerage firm in Bangalore. With a mission to make trading affordable and accessible, Zerodha disrupted the traditional brokerage model in India. Nikhil, coming from a middle-class background, faced challenges in the initial stages, but his innovative approach, coupled with technology-driven solutions, led Zerodha to become one of the largest retail stockbrokers in India. The platform's simplicity and low-cost structure appealed to a new generation of investors, contributing to its remarkable success

Byju Raveendran, a former teacher from a small town in Kerala, started Byju's in 2011 as an edtech platform. Beginning with offline coaching classes, Byju's transitioned to an online model, providing interactive learning content. Facing initial struggles to gain trust and acceptance for online education, Byju's persisted, and with the rise of digital learning, it became a prominent name in the Indian education sector. Byju's success story reflects the impact of leveraging technology to revolutionize traditional educational methods.

Flipkart, founded in 2007 by Sachin and Binny Bansal, began as an online bookstore operating out of a small apartment in Bangalore. The founders faced numerous challenges, from personally delivering books to navigating a nascent e-commerce market. However, their relentless commitment and innovative strategies, such as cash-on-delivery, propelled Flipkart into becoming one of India's largest e-commerce platforms, offering a diverse range of products.

Paytm, founded in 2010 by Vijay Shekhar Sharma, started as a mobile recharge and bill payment platform. Operating in a market dominated by cash transactions, Paytm navigated challenges by pioneering digital wallets and later expanding into various financial services. Vijay Shekhar Sharma's vision and adaptability contributed to Paytm's transformation into one of India's leading digital payment and financial services providers.
